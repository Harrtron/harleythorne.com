Nudge

A gentle app for couples who want to feel more connected

What Is Nudge?

Nudge is a gentle app for couples who want to feel more connected while getting everyday life done.

It's designed for real relationships — especially when one partner struggles to get started, remember things, or follow through, and the other ends up feeling like they're always asking or reminding.

Instead of nagging or pressure, Nudge turns small actions into moments of connection.

How It Works

One partner adds simple tasks ("Things") they'd like help with. The other completes them in a satisfying, ADHD-friendly way — by scribbling them out, one at a time.

When something's done, it's visible. When effort happens, it's noticed. And when it's noticed, it can be appreciated.

Alongside tasks, Nudge also creates gentle "Moments" — small prompts for kindness or connection — like a hug, a smile, or a few minutes together. These aren't chores. They're just little reminders that relationships are built in the in-between.

Why Nudge Is Different

Nudge isn't about productivity, fairness, or tracking who does more.

There are no streaks, no scores, no guilt, no dashboards.

It's built around a simple idea: when effort feels good and gets noticed, people show up more naturally.

That's especially powerful for couples where ADHD, overwhelm, or busy lives make communication harder than it needs to be.

What Nudge Helps With

  • Reducing nagging and tension
  • Making effort visible without arguments
  • Helping partners feel appreciated
  • Turning small wins into positive momentum
  • Building connection in a busy world

It's not therapy. It's not a to-do list. It's a kinder way to support each other.

Who It's For

Nudge is for couples who love each other, get stuck on the small stuff, want things to feel lighter not heavier, and are tired of arguing about chores or follow-through.

Especially when one partner struggles more with initiation or organisation.

The Idea Behind It

Nudge was inspired by a simple truth: crossing something off a list together feels better than talking about why it didn't get done.

Nudge helps couples replace pressure with progress — and progress with connection.
