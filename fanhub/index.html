FanHub

Loyalty platform that rewarded real-world fandom

50k+ MAU 200k+ users $3m+ funding

Overview

FanHub is a loyalty and engagement platform built for football fans — rewarding real-world fandom and turning matchday behaviour into measurable value for clubs and brands.

As co-founder and Chief Product Officer, I helped build FanHub from zero to a live consumer platform used by tens of thousands of fans every month. The core idea is simple but ambitious: fans prove their loyalty by turning up, predicting games, and choosing the right places on matchday — and we reward them for it.

Features in Action

Stadium check-ins with geolocation

Earning LYLTY currency through engagement

Match predictions and leaderboards

Mystery rewards and redemption

Stadium grounds and venue information

End-of-season fan statistics

How It Worked

The product blended consumer engagement mechanics with a performance-driven commercial model underneath:

  • ●Check-ins: Stadium check-ins proven with geolocation
  • ●Predictions: Fans prove their knowledge and competed against others on leaderboards, including lineup and results predictions
  • ●Mystery rewards: Surprise incentives to drive discovery and delight
  • ●Progression systems: Tiered mechanics that rewarded consistency
  • ●LYLTY currency: A proprietary blockchain-based points system fans earned and redeemed

What I Led

I focused on building a contagious app — one that fans wanted to share, talk about, and bring their friends into.

  • ●Rapid organic growth: Drove viral growth across social platforms by understanding what made fans want to share — their predictions, their leaderboard positions, their rewards
  • ●User psychology: Applied deep understanding of fan behaviour to design features that felt natural, rewarding, and worth talking about
  • ●Contagious mechanics: Built systems that turned every fan action into a potential share moment, creating momentum that spread organically
  • ●Bold experiments: Did things that seemed crazy at the time — like launching a waitlist that created scarcity and demand, turning "you can't get in yet" into "you need to get in"
  • ●Team building: Hired nearly every person and built the entire product and tech team from scratch
  • ●Tech culture: Created a culture of high velocity yet stable outcome-driven tech delivery
  • ●Content strategy: Led content strategy for long periods — our socials were popping

What I Learned

Building FanHub taught me how hard — and how rewarding — it is to ship real products in ambiguous conditions:

  • ●Balancing user delight with commercial reality
  • ●Simplifying complex systems for mass-market users
  • ●Taking full ownership of outcomes rather than hiding behind process

It's the foundation of how I think about product today: start with human behaviour, design systems that create momentum, and only build things that can survive contact with the real world.
